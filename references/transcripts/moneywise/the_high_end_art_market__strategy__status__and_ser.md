## The Art Collector's Strategy [02:30]

**Jackie Lampart:** Back in the 80s, there was an art collector by the name of Eli Saka, and he was one of the many fortunate people who made a lot of money in the art market. However, his fortune was, well, calculated. When you buy and sell expensive art, there is a very thorough verification process, and it's very difficult to fake. However, Saka realized he didn't actually have to fake that process. He made a habit of legitimately buying high-profile art, and that would get him everything he needed—the whole package. The certificate of authenticity, the bill of sale, the history. And to any prospective buyer, it would seem as if they were getting the real thing, except they weren't. And the reason they weren't is because he would make very convincing reproductions of the art, sometimes like five or six at a time, and he would sell them to buyers in East Asia and give them all the paperwork. So, to them, it was like completely legit. They had the paperwork to back it up. So yes, he's making like 5x on his investment, and he's never actually selling the asset. But there are ways to make money in the art market that don't involve forfeiting that money or serving jail time.
