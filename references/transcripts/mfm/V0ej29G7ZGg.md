It's a paradox that many job seekers face today. The job market is tough, and it's even tougher when you're trying to break into a new field. But what if there was a way to bypass the traditional hiring process? What if you could prove your skills before you even get an interview? That's where the "portfolio-first" approach comes in. Instead of just sending out resumes, you build a portfolio of work that demonstrates your abilities. This could be anything from a blog post, a code snippet, a design project, or even a simple video. By showing, not just telling, you're giving employers a tangible reason to hire you. It's a shift from "I can do this" to "Here's what I've done." And in a world where everyone is competing for the same jobs, this kind of proof is invaluable. So, if you're feeling stuck, stop focusing on the resume and start building your proof.
