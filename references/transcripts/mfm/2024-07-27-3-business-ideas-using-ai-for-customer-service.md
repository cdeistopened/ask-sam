## AI Voice Agents for Small Businesses [00:00:00]

**Shaan:** I think there are going to be a bunch of interesting voice services for everything from restaurants to HVAC companies to dental reception that are just like — answer the phone. That's one of the ideas we had. It could be informational, like "we are open from 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM," or it could be a lead generation business where someone says "my plumbing broke — are you available tomorrow at 3?"

**Sam:** I'm a huge believer in this. Huge.

**Shaan:** It's like when the internet came out, one of the obvious things was: every restaurant just needs their menu online. Put up that your restaurant exists, where it's located, and your menu — even as a PDF. That's still value-add. It became where every business needed a website.

Now I think what's going to happen is that every business needs an agent. So what's the agent for most small businesses?

I call pest control — we always get mice jumping in our pool for some reason — and nobody ever picks up the phone. Because it's usually Mike's Pest Control, and Mike's out in the field all day actually doing the work. He doesn't pick up the phone. So you leave a message, and then you call ten of them because you're not sure Mike's going to get back to you. Whoever gets back to you first wins the business.

Mike loses business because he doesn't pick up the phone. But he's also not going to hire somebody to just sit there and wait for the three calls a day he's going to get. That wouldn't make sense.

## Building a Phone Agent on Bland.ai [00:02:30]

**Shaan:** So I built one of these in our AI weekly tutoring session. We have the same problem for our offshore recruiting business. We own a company called Somewhere — the idea is you can find amazing talent, they're just somewhere out in the world, you just have to find them.

If you go to Somewhere.com, you say "I'm looking for a designer" or "I need someone to get me leads for my marketing business" or "I need someone for data entry." All these jobs. But the button on the site is basically: fill out this form and we'll get back to you.

No matter how many sales agents we have, "a call tomorrow" is not as good as "talk to me right now." Because right now is when you're interested. Right now is when you're on the website. Right now is when you're not thinking about other ways to solve the problem. That's when you have the opportunity to sell them.

**Shaan:** So — have you seen Bland.ai?

**Sam:** Assume no, and that my mind is being blown by all of this.

**Shaan:** Bland.ai lets you build a phone agent for yourself. I went on there and built an agent that answers the phone when somebody goes to Somewhere and wants to hire someone. It'll say: "Awesome, what are you hiring for? Have you ever hired overseas?"

You say yeah. It says: "Cool, tell me what you're looking for in a couple sentences." You tell it. Then it says: "It sounds like you're looking for a Shopify developer. Our usual budget for that is $2,000 a month — does that work, or are you looking for something a little more, a little less?"

Then it does the intake — the initial sales call — and says: "No problem. We've hired for 85 other Shopify brands this month who needed Shopify developers. You're in good hands. I'm going to start looking for candidates and email you tomorrow with three options. How does that sound?"

The person says great. Or the agent pulls from our existing database and says: "Here's an example resume — is this the type of person you'd want?" Yes or no.

## The Human Handoff and Future Vision [00:05:00]

**Shaan:** So the human salesperson comes into work and sees a ticket: "The AI agent did the initial sales call, found the customer's requirements, warmed up the sale, and told the customer what they needed to know." All the stuff you repeat every time on the phone. Now the human can follow up with a more bespoke answer for that specific person.

That's the future I see. I wasn't able to fully build it — I did a prototype — but that's what I think websites, even ours which is an internet business, should have. Which means every plumbing company, every pest control company, every restaurant is going to have their version of that.

**Sam:** This is 100% way better than having a call center. Or it will be, once it works as well as it needs to. But this is absolutely the way to go.
